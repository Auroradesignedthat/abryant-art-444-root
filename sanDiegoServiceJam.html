Challenge

An impromptu team was formed and were given 48 hours to develop a product that addresses a universal problem solely based on the theme “outside the box”. The solution needed to easy to obtain while offering an unconventional approach to a common problem: improving healthy communication within families.

Process

  • Interviewed target demographic about observations on the topic and pain points.
  • Used “Crazy Eights” process to develop paper prototypes
  • Conducted field usability tests.
  • Reiterated designs based on feedback from the participants.
  • Retested new versions of the product.

Outcome

A working prototype that was well-received by interviewees was developed in 48 hours by an interdisciplinary team.

"Thinking Outside the Box"

Our group brainstormed outside-the-box ideas and ultimately created a new, innovative way to communicate with families.

Everything Starts at Home

So we realized that communication at home also translates to communication in public spaces, and our prototype was developed with this in mind.

Main Point

We solved the challenge of collaborating with unfamiliar team members by using listening, empathy, and communication to unite our strengths and effectively work together.

Keeping Our Points Simple

  • We brainstormed solutions for family communication and ultimately decided to create an engaging card game.
  • We interviewed five families at Liberty Station about communication and how parents listen during problem-solving.
  • I facilitated turning ideas into simplified card wireframes, forming the foundation and rules for our game.

Impact

  • We successfully created a prototype of a working game which fosters communication in a family dynamic.
  • Our new interdisciplinary team quickly united to solve a problem together.
  • Our team stayed connected long after the project ended.

Key Takeaways

From Clarity to Creation

Developing and using a clear, step-by-step process from general to specific enabled us to create a working prototype that addresses a complex, ambiguous problem.

Lasting Connections, Lasting Growth

Collaborative communication and problem-solving foster lasting bonds that extend beyond the project, significantly enhancing professional growth.
